ASCE 7-16 Exposure Categories, Explained Clearly

The code wording is dense, but the idea is simple: look at what the wind travels over before it reaches the building. Start at the building, look upwind, and decide whether that upwind terrain behaves like Exposure B, C, or D for the required distance.

Exposure B

Built-up / rough terrain

Use B only if you can prove enough Surface Roughness B exists continuously upwind.

If h ≤ 30 ft: more than 1,500 ft upwind

If h > 30 ft: more than max(2,600 ft, 20h)

Exposure C

Default / open terrain

Use C when the site does not qualify for B or D.

This is the catch-all category, so many ordinary buildings land here unless B can be clearly justified.

Exposure D

Very open terrain / water

Use D when very open D terrain exists far enough upwind, or when the building is still close enough downwind from that D condition.

D fetch: more than max(5,000 ft, 20h)

1) The main idea

Read the sketch from left to right. The wind comes from the left and reaches the building on the right.

2) Simple decision process

  1. Pick a wind direction.
  2. Stand at the building and look upwind.
  3. Classify the terrain in that direction as Surface Roughness B, C, or D.
  4. Check whether it extends far enough upwind.
  5. If B qualifies, use Exposure B.
  6. If D qualifies, use Exposure D.
  7. If neither qualifies, use Exposure C.
  8. If the site sits in a transition zone, use the category that produces the larger wind forces unless a recognized rational analysis supports an intermediate value.
Exposure B
• if h ≤ 30 ft: B terrain > 1,500 ft upwind
• if h > 30 ft: B terrain > max(2,600 ft, 20h)

Exposure D
• D terrain > max(5,000 ft, 20h) upwind
• or the building is close enough downwind from D terrain

Exposure C
• use when B and D do not apply

3) The commonly confusing part about Exposure D

The building does not need to sit directly on a shoreline or other D terrain to be Exposure D. Even if the immediate terrain right next to the building is B or C, Exposure D can still control when the building is close enough downwind of a D condition.

That distance check is max(600 ft, 20h).

Plain-English version: a nearby very open area can still “count” even if a short strip of more ordinary terrain sits between that area and the building.

The “near a D condition” sketch

Bottom line

Exposure B is a category you must justify with enough rough terrain upwind. Exposure D is triggered by very open terrain or being close enough downwind from it. Exposure C is the default whenever B and D do not apply.
